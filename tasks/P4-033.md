## Problem

The logic analyzer on the DIN MIDI OUT (GPIO20) caught the 24-PPQN clock **stalling
and dropping pulses**. In one 8-second capture:

- one **114 ms gap** in the `0xF8` stream (~8 missing clocks),
- **6 catch-up bursts** where two `0xF8` bytes went out **0.32 ms apart** (one MIDI
  byte time at 31250 — the UART shifting them back-to-back).

An earlier capture had an **853 ms** stall. Nothing in the serial log looked wrong;
the device "worked". Only the wire showed it.

## Root cause

`clock_out_task` — the 1 ms real-time clock generator — called `ESP_LOGx`. **The ESP
console is a BLOCKING UART write:** at 115200 baud a ~110-char line costs **7.5–12 ms**.

Proven, not guessed. A per-stage tick probe (`gap` = scheduling delay, `work` = time
inside the loop) reported:

```
max_work=9135us | meas=3 link=1 cfg=7 tick=14 midi=2 metro=1 led=2 statlog=7537
```

Every real stage was single-digit microseconds; **the status log alone was 7537 us**.
`gap` stayed ~1 ms, so it was never scheduler starvation — the task blocked on itself.

Three logs sat in the hot path:

| log | rate | why it hurt |
|---|---|---|
| status line | 1/sec | stalled the clock once a second |
| metronome click | every beat (2–3/sec) | stalled 2–3x/sec whenever the metronome ran |
| **transport START/STOP** | on transition | fired **on the bar line** — stalls exactly at the downbeat |

Because catch-up is capped by `MAX_BURST`, the stalled pulses were **dropped**, not
merely delayed.

### Observer effect (worth remembering)

The *first* version of the probe logged on every overrun. That log blocked ~13 ms,
which delayed the next tick, which logged again — a feedback loop reporting **3945
"overruns" in 40 s**. It manufactured the very fault it measured. Rate-limiting the
probe to 1/sec revealed the true signal: exactly **1 overrun/sec**, matching the
1/sec status log.

## Fix

`clock_out_task` no longer calls `ESP_LOGx` at all.

- Publish plain scalars to a `static volatile s_stat` struct (peers/bpm/locked/usb/
  playing/pulses/clicks + tick-health maxima). Plain stores, microseconds.
- New `status_task` (priority **2**, below clock_out's 6) owns the console and prints
  once a second. A blocking UART write can never preempt the clock generator.
- Metronome click: counted, not logged. Transport: not logged (the web UI's transport
  pill already shows it).
- The tick-health probe (`gap` / `work` / `overruns`) stays in **permanently** — it
  costs two `esp_timer_get_time()` reads per tick and it is what caught this.

## Verification (on the wire, not the log)

| | BEFORE (8 s) | AFTER (15 s) |
|---|---|---|
| catch-up bursts (<1 ms apart) | 6 | **0** |
| stalls (>40 ms gap) | 1 × 114 ms | **0** |
| clock spacing | — | 14.881 ms, 331 µs stdev |

Zero dropouts over a window ~2x longer than the one that previously caught a stall.
Serial confirms `max_work` fell **12109 us → 1303 us** (and that residual was a
one-off during boot/WiFi bring-up).

## Notes

- The residual **331 µs** jitter is the 1 kHz FreeRTOS tick granularity. It moves DIN
  and USB *together* (same emit point), so the outputs stay locked to each other.
  Tightening it means generating the clock from a hardware timer — a separate project.
- **Rule for this codebase: never call `ESP_LOGx` from `clock_out_task`.** Publish to
  `s_stat` and let `status_task` print.

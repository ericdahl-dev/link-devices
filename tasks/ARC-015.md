## Problem

`KitchenSync/main/ks_main.c:67-228` (`clock_out_task`) is a single ~160-line `while(1)`
that per iteration does: measurement poll, timeline read + session gate, beat basis
(`beat_source_step`), transport-playing gate, the `reprime`+config-gen fold + transport
reset, per-output MIDI clock fan-out, transport Start/Stop fan-out, metronome update,
LED render (own ~50 fps throttle), 1 Hz status log, optional phase trace.

The *pieces* are deep tested modules (`beat_source`, `clock_output`, `transport`,
`metronome`, `metro_strip`, `usb_midi_pack`). What's untested is exactly the
**orchestration**: the ordering, the shared `beats` fanned to clock+metronome+LED, the
reprime fold (two unrelated signals: `bs.reprime` and the config-gen counter), and the
throttle bookkeeping (`last_led`, `last_log`, `led_showing`, `seen_gen`) interleaved
100 lines apart. Low locality.

## Solution (deepen)

A pure `ks_tick_step(state, inputs) → plan` where
`inputs = {have_session, xform, tl, t_now, cfg snapshot, transport flags}` and
`plan = {pulses-per-cable to emit, transport action, click?, led-frame request?,
reprime?}`. The task loop becomes: gather inputs → step → execute plan (send USB,
click, show LED). The reprime fold, the "shared beat" contract, and the tp_playing
gating become host-testable; the loop drops to thin I/O.

## Problem

Dan's KitchenSync Touch is, right now, a **bare development board with wires soldered to a header**.
That is what a bench prototype looks like. It is not what a thing you hand to a musician looks
like, and the difference is not cosmetic.

The specific failure mode is mechanical and predictable: **the DIN cable is soldered directly to
GPIO11 and ground on the board header.** Any tug on that cable -- a foot, a case lid, coiling it at
the end of a night -- puts the full force on the solder joint and the pad underneath it. Pads lift.
When that one lifts, the device silently stops sending MIDI, the failure looks exactly like a
firmware bug, and it is unfixable in the field.

This project has spent an entire cycle proving that the *firmware* does not drop MIDI pulses. It
would be a bleak outcome to ship a unit that drops them because a wire moved.

## Scope

- [ ] Printed enclosure for the Waveshare S3-Touch-LCD-1.47, with the screen and the USB-C port
      accessible. Touch is the **only** control surface -- the device deliberately has no settings
      screen on the LCD (an accidental tap must never pop a config screen mid-performance,
      ESP-016), so the enclosure has no buttons to accommodate. It just has to not block the glass.
- [ ] **Strain relief on the DIN cable.** This is the actual point of the ticket. The cable must be
      anchored to the *enclosure*, so that force lands on plastic and not on a solder pad. A
      through-hole and a knot is enough; a proper clamp is better. What matters is that the load
      path never reaches GPIO11.
- [ ] A panel-mount 5-pin DIN jack is the better answer than a captive pigtail if it fits -- it
      makes the cable replaceable and the failure recoverable by anyone with a spare MIDI lead.
      Prefer it unless the depth does not allow it.
- [ ] Label the DIN jack **OUT**. It is an output only.
- [ ] Ventilation. The board runs warm (asked about and dismissed as normal, but a sealed box is a
      different thermal case than an open bench).
- [ ] Confirm the enclosure does not sit over the antenna in a way that hurts WiFi -- the S3 runs
      WiFi on-die and the device is useless without Link.

## Wiring reference (already established, do not re-derive)

- **DIN MIDI TX = GPIO11** on the S3-Touch-LCD-1.47 free header.
- MIDI DIN pins run physically **1 · 4 · 2 · 5 · 3**. **Pin 5 = data, pin 2 = ground, pin 4 = +5 V.**
  (Pin 4 reads flat high on an analyzer and has already cost one wasted capture. Getting it wrong
  in *solder* is worse than getting it wrong on a probe.)

## Coupled to ESP-020

Ship this unit with OTA (ESP-020) and it can be fixed remotely for the rest of its life. Ship it
without, and every future fix requires the box back. Ship it with a fragile solder joint and it
may come back anyway, for a reason no firmware update can address.

The two tickets are the same decision seen from two ends: **can we still help Dan after the box
leaves the room?**

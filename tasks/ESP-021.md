## Problem

Dan's KitchenSync Touch is, right now, a **bare development board with wires soldered to a header**.
That is what a bench prototype looks like. It is not what a thing you hand to a musician looks
like, and the difference is not cosmetic.

The specific failure mode is mechanical and predictable: **the DIN cable is soldered directly to
GPIO11 and ground on the board header.** Any tug on that cable -- a foot, a case lid, coiling it at
the end of a night -- puts the full force on the solder joint and the pad underneath it. Pads lift.
When that one lifts, the device silently stops sending MIDI, the failure looks exactly like a
firmware bug, and it is unfixable in the field.

This project has spent an entire cycle proving that the *firmware* does not drop MIDI pulses. It
would be a bleak outcome to ship a unit that drops them because a wire moved.

## Scope

- [ ] Printed enclosure for the Waveshare S3-Touch-LCD-1.47, with the screen and the USB-C port
      accessible. Touch is the **only** control surface -- the device deliberately has no settings
      screen on the LCD (an accidental tap must never pop a config screen mid-performance,
      ESP-016), so the enclosure has no buttons to accommodate. It just has to not block the glass.
- [ ] **Panel-mount 3.5mm TRS jack for MIDI OUT** (connector decision, 2026-07-11 -- supersedes the
      captive DIN pigtail this ticket originally scoped). A panel-mount jack *is* the strain relief:
      the load path lands on the panel, not on GPIO11's solder pad, and a chewed cable is replaced
      by anyone with a spare lead instead of coming back to us. It is also far shallower than a
      5-pin DIN, so it actually fits the S3-Touch enclosure.
- [ ] **Strain relief on the internal wiring.** The jack solves the *external* pull. Still dress and
      anchor the short run from the jack back to the header so that nothing tugs GPIO11 from inside.
      What matters is unchanged: the load path never reaches GPIO11.
- [ ] Label the jack **OUT**. It is an output only.
- [ ] **Ship a Type-A TRS -> 5-pin DIN (male) adapter cable in the box.** Dan's RC-505 has a DIN
      MIDI IN (ESP-016), so the unit is useless to him without the adapter. Two of them: one to use,
      one to lose.
- [ ] Ventilation. The board runs warm (asked about and dismissed as normal, but a sealed box is a
      different thermal case than an open bench).
- [ ] Confirm the enclosure does not sit over the antenna in a way that hurts WiFi -- the S3 runs
      WiFi on-die and the device is useless without Link.

## Wiring reference (already established, do not re-derive)

- **MIDI TX = GPIO11** on the S3-Touch-LCD-1.47 free header.
- MIDI OUT needs **no optocoupler** -- isolation is the *receiver's* job. It is two resistors, the
  jack, and the TX pin. Nothing else.

### The 3.5mm TRS jack -- wire it as **Type A**

TRS-MIDI has two incompatible standards. **Type A is the MIDI Association standard (2018)** and is
what current gear uses. **Type B swaps Tip and Ring and will pass no MIDI at all.** It is not a
subtle degradation -- it is simply dead, and it looks exactly like a firmware bug. Wire Type A:

| TRS | is DIN pin | connect to | through |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tip** | 5 (data) | **GPIO11** (UART TX) | **10 Ω** |
| **Ring** | 4 (+V) | **3V3** | **33 Ω** |
| **Sleeve** | 2 (ground) | **GND** | -- |

- Resistors are **10 Ω** and **33 Ω** (on hand: 1/2 W, ±1%). These are the 3.3 V values -- the classic
  220 Ω/220 Ω pair is sized for 5 V logic and runs the current loop marginally low from a 3V3 TX.
- If it is ever built backwards, the fix is swapping **Tip and Ring** -- nothing else.
- The DIN end (on the adapter cable) still runs physically **1 · 4 · 2 · 5 · 3**. Pin 4 reads flat
  high on an analyzer and has already cost one wasted capture. Getting it wrong in *solder* is worse
  than getting it wrong on a probe.

## Coupled to ESP-020

Ship this unit with OTA (ESP-020) and it can be fixed remotely for the rest of its life. Ship it
without, and every future fix requires the box back. Ship it with a fragile solder joint and it
may come back anyway, for a reason no firmware update can address.

The two tickets are the same decision seen from two ends: **can we still help Dan after the box
leaves the room?**

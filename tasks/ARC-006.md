**Done (PR #24):** pure `ks_form` owns URL-decode + checkbox-absence + the
grammar replay behind a host-tested seam (`test_ks_form`); `save_handler` is
thin glue. Later extended in P4-015 with `ks_form_apply` (patch semantics) for
the live endpoint.


## Problem (architecture review candidate A)

`KitchenSync/main/ks_web.cpp` `save_handler` carries real, fiddly logic with **no
host test** ‚Äî an ADR-0003 violation:
- `url_decode` (hand-rolled `%XX`/`+` decoder of untrusted POST bytes), untested.
- "unchecked checkbox absent ‚Üí off" reconciliation (`saw_clock_out`/`saw_metronome`/
  `saw_out_en[]`), untested ‚Äî the part most likely to regress (add a 5th output,
  forget a `saw_` flag ‚Üí silently force-disabled).
- The `clk<N>_en` index detection **duplicates the `clk<N>_<field>` grammar** already
  parsed (and tested) in `ks_config.c:ks_config_set` ‚Äî two parsers of one
  grammar, one tested, that must stay in lockstep. `build_outputs` *emits* the same
  field names as a third, implicit contract.

## Solution

A pure, host-tested `ks_form.c`: `resolve(raw_body, base_cfg) -> cfg` owning
decode + pair-split + absent‚Üíoff + the field grammar. `save_handler` shrinks to
recv ‚Üí resolve ‚Üí validate ‚Üí NVS ‚Üí reboot. `build_outputs` and the resolver draw
field names from one source of truth so they can't drift.

## Deliverable

Pure `ks_form.{c,h}` (in `X32Link/` or a shared dir) with `test_ks_form`
covering url-decode edge cases, checkbox-off, index bounds, and a build‚Üíparse
round-trip. `save_handler` reduced to glue. Host suite + `idf.py build` green.

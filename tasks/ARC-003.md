## Goal

Route every "a bar is 4 beats" assumption through one tiny deep helper that
knows a bar is `quantum_beats`, the way `app_config.c` already owns slot
ranges. Non-4/4 quanta then just work.

## Problem (from the architecture review)

"One bar" is hardcoded as `4 *` in at least two places:
- `bpm_publisher.c:33` — `(uint32_t)(4 * s_refresh_bars * 60000.0f / bpm)`
- `X32MidiClock.ino:40` — `if (++beat_count >= 4 * MCK_REFRESH_BARS)`

But `g_config.quantum_beats` is user-configurable 1–16 (`app_config.h:20`). A 3-
or 7-beat bar silently uses the wrong refresh/resend cadence. The literal `4`
is a latent bug — the same "one constant, two meanings" shape as the
`MCK_CLOCK_WINDOW` overload already fixed.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Pure helper (e.g. in a small `bar.{h,c}` or folded into an existing pure
      module): `bar_beats(quantum)`, `bar_pulses(quantum)` (= 24*quantum),
      `bar_ms(bpm, quantum)`. Host-tested, edge cases for quantum < 1.
- [ ] `bpm_publisher.c:33` and `X32MidiClock.ino:40` route through it — no bare
      `4 *` left for a bar.
- [ ] Refresh/resend cadence verified correct for a non-4 quantum (e.g. 3, 7).
- [ ] Host suite green; both firmwares compile.

## Notes

- Cheap, safe, high-signal — a good warm-up before the larger ARC-001/002.
- `midi_phase_calc`/`midi_phase_valid` already use `24 * quantum` correctly —
  reuse that convention.

## Vision

One device that speaks tempo fluently in every direction: **Ableton Link**,
**MIDI clock/transport** (DIN + USB), and **analog clock** (Eurorack / DIN-sync) —
each protocol usable as an **input** (the device follows) or an **output** (the
device drives), with a **master-clock option** where the device is the
authoritative tempo source and everything else locks to it.

Today the family is a *follower-and-bridge*: it receives a Link session and
re-emits MIDI clock (and, on the roadmap, analog). The north star is a
*clock hub* — put it at the centre of a rig and every other box, modular or
software, syncs to it, whether the tempo originates from Link, from an external
clock, from audio, or from the device's own internal/tap clock.

This ticket is the umbrella. It does not implement anything itself — it names the
shape so the atomic tickets below aim at one coherent target instead of drifting.
Product framing lives in `docs/kitchensync-gtm.md` (this is Approach C, the P4
flagship, made explicit); ADR-0004 (web = full config, touch = real-time) and
ADR-0008 (P4 is the UX reference) still govern how it's exposed.

## The capability matrix

The "full-featured" claim decomposes into IN and OUT for each protocol, plus the
source that feeds OUT:

| Protocol | IN (follow) | OUT (drive) |
|---|---|---|
| **Ableton Link** | join session, follow peer tempo/phase *(done)* | originate/set session tempo; be transport controller |
| **MIDI clock** | clock IN → session tempo (P4-011) | 0xF8 clock + Start/Stop/Continue + Song Position, as master |
| **Analog** | clock/reset IN (decode a pulse train) | Eurorack / DIN-sync pulse + reset out (P4-021, LNK-028) |
| **Audio** | mic / line tempo-follow (P4-020 done, P4-026) | — |

**Master-clock option** = the arbitrated tempo source is *internal* — manual BPM,
tap tempo, or a held/latched external tempo — and every OUT is driven from it, so
the device leads the rig rather than following anything.

## The seam this needs

One **tempo-source arbiter**: choose the authoritative source from
{Link peer, MIDI-in, audio-follow, internal/tap/manual}, and fan it out to every
enabled OUT (Link tempo, MIDI clock+transport, analog) through the shared
derivation. ESP-007 started this ("publish detected tempo into Link + tempo-source
arbitration"); ARC-019 (one clock-out derivation for all firmwares) is the OUT
side of the same seam. Getting these two right is what makes "master or follow, any
protocol" a config choice instead of a firmware fork.

## Rolls up (existing atomic tickets)

- **Tempo source / arbitration:** ESP-007 (follow-beat → Link + arbitration),
  P4-020 *(done)* audio follow, P4-026 (line-input sync decode).
- **MIDI:** P4-011 (clock IN → Link), ESP-015 *(done)* DIN OUT, LNK-027 *(done)*
  USB-MIDI clock OUT, ESP-011 *(done)* transport buttons.
- **Analog:** P4-021 (per-output analog pulse/clock), LNK-028 (Eurorack sync out).
- **Transport / musical time:** ESP-010 (start/stop the Link session),
  P4-008 *(done)* transport OUT from play state, P4-022 (configurable meter),
  P4-025 (transport modes: quantize/resync + latency offset).
- **Foundation the hub rides on:** ARC-018 (one timing core), ARC-019 (one
  clock-out derivation) — without these, "master clock across three protocols"
  gets re-implemented per firmware.

## Master-clock: what's genuinely new

Most OUT paths exist or are ticketed; the *new* concept is the device as tempo
**origin**, which needs:

1. **Internal clock source** — set BPM (web/touch), **tap tempo**, and a "hold
   current tempo" latch, feeding the arbiter with no external input.
2. **Source selection + priority** — auto (follow whatever's present) vs. locked
   (master), with clear arbitration when several inputs are live. One owner
   (extends ESP-007), host-tested.
3. **Transport master** — originate Start/Stop/Continue + Song Position Pointer,
   quantized; others follow the device's transport.
4. **Link as leader** — set the session tempo and act as transport controller
   rather than passively following a peer.

## Open questions

- Is "master clock" a global mode, or per-output (e.g. drive analog from internal
  while still following Link)? The arbiter design turns on this.
- Which hardware tier is the flagship hub — P4 (Pro) only, or does Touch get a
  cut-down master mode? (ESP-017 says the two differ only in hardware/exposure.)
- Analog IN (decoding an external pulse train) — in scope, or OUT-only first?
- Clock resolution / jitter budget as master (the P4-033 blocking-log and ESP-018
  DIN-stall bugs show the RT loop is the constraint) — measure on the logic
  analyzer before claiming "master-grade."

## Definition of done (epic-level)

The device can, from one config surface, be set to follow **or** master on each of
Link / MIDI / analog independently, with tempo originating from any of
{Link, MIDI-in, audio, internal/tap/manual}, and drive every enabled output in
sync — verified end-to-end on hardware with the logic analyzer, not by ear.

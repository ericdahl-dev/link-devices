## Goal

Derive tempo from **audio picked up by the onboard microphone** and drive the Link
session (and every output) from it — so a stage full of gear can follow a live
drummer / percussionist whose tempo drifts naturally. The differentiating feature.

## Inspiration

JMK CLOCKstep:MULTI "Follow Beat / Human Groove Tracking" — tracks live drums in
real time and continuously updates tempo (https://jmkmusicpedals.com/products/clockstep-multi).
Their box does it from MIDI or audio; ours would do it from the mic, feeding the
**Ableton Link** session (which their box doesn't even have).

## Why it fits our hardware

The P4-NANO mic runs into the **ES8311 codec** we already drive for the metronome
(I2S; DIN = GPIO11, currently unused). The dual RISC-V cores have ample headroom
for onset detection + tempo estimation; the NPU is a later option.

## Scope

- Glue: capture the mic via I2S/ES8311 record path (codec already inited for the
  metronome — add the input direction).
- Pure, host-tested DSP: onset detection (energy/flux) -> inter-onset intervals ->
  tempo estimate (autocorrelation or interval clustering) with smoothing +
  confidence. Feed clean audio buffers in; get {bpm, confidence, beat_phase} out.
- Wire into the tempo-source arbiter (ties to P4-011 s2): when audio-follow is
  selected and confident, it becomes the master and publishes tempo into Link.
- Web UI: a source selector + a live "detected audio BPM / confidence" readout
  (like the MIDI-In row).

## Deliverable

Clap/drum into the mic and the Link session tempo tracks it; other Link peers and
all clock outputs follow. Pure DSP host-tested; verified on hardware against a
metronome source and a live player.

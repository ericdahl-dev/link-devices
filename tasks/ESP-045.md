## Why (the Mini's one live action needs to be physical)

The v1 product is the cheap MIDI-only "Mini" (headless S3 Super Mini, 1 DIN out, app for config).
The office-hours design locks **one physical button** as its live-performance surface: everything
else is the app, but *play/stop and realign must be a real button you can hit mid-set* without
reaching for a phone. Today the Super Mini has **no button** — transport comes only over
`/transport` + `/live` from the app. That's fine for config, not for performance.

The firmware is most of the way there and it is NOT new logic:

- `KitchenSyncTouch.ino` already has a `#ifdef HAS_BUTTONS` block (the ESP-025 bench rig): debounced
  `Button` inputs post the SAME `TransportLaunchIntent` into the SAME `ktouch_transport` mailbox the
  touch UI uses, so the 1 ms writer is identical — what the bench proved is what the Mini runs.
- `buttons.c` gives `button_reset` / `button_update` (debounce).
- Intents exist: `TL_INTENT_PLAY`, `TL_INTENT_STOP`, `TL_INTENT_REALIGN`.
- Status feedback already exists: **ESP-043** drives the onboard WS2812 (GPIO48) via
  `ktouch_status_rgb` (dark stopped / amber armed / green running / cyan Link-locked). So the box
  can already *show* armed — this ticket only adds the *input*.

So this is a **wiring + config-flag + one-button-gesture** job, not a new transport path.

## The gap vs. what exists

The bench `HAS_BUTTONS` block is **two** buttons (transport + realign) on **GPIO32/33** — a
classic-ESP32 DevKit pinout. The Mini is different in two ways:

1. **One button, two gestures.** Product decision: a single button. **Short press = play/stop**
   (quantized, arms on the bar line), **hold (~600 ms) = realign**. Needs hold-detection on top of
   `button_update` (short vs long press), which the bench two-button block does not have.
2. **S3 GPIOs, not classic-ESP32.** GPIO32/33 don't exist the same way; pick a **safe exposed S3
   input**. Avoid: 0/3/45/46 (strapping), 19/20 (USB), 43/44 (UART0), **48 (onboard RGB — ESP-043)**,
   **11 (DIN MIDI TX — `din_midi_out.h`)**. Use an exposed non-strapping pin with a real internal
   pull-up, wired button→GND, `INPUT_PULLUP` (pressed = LOW), matching the bench convention.

## Scope

- Define `HAS_BUTTONS` (or a narrower `HAS_TRANSPORT_BUTTON`) for the **Super Mini** build in
  `tools/build-supermini.sh` / `config.h`, without dragging in the two-button/LED-pin bench rig.
- Add a **single-button transport gesture**: debounce → short-press posts PLAY/STOP (toggle on
  `ktouch_transport_state()`), long-press posts `TL_INTENT_REALIGN`. Keep the mailbox post exactly
  as the bench does — never touch the wire from `loop()`; the writer fires on the grid.
- Status stays on the **onboard RGB (ESP-043)** — no new LED wiring for the plain-button build.
  (If a ChromaTek 19mm WS2812 button is used instead, its LED can chain as pixel #2 off the WS2812
  line and mirror the same `ktouch_status_rgb` — an enclosure-dependent option, not required here.)

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Short press on the Super Mini arms/stops transport (quantized), identical to an app
      `/transport` PLAY/STOP — verified by the armed→running transition being the device's, not
      client-predicted.
- [ ] Hold (~600 ms) posts a realign (`0xFC`+`0xFA` on the bar line), distinct from a short press.
- [ ] The chosen GPIO is a safe S3 input (not strapping/USB/UART0/48/11); button→GND + `INPUT_PULLUP`.
- [ ] Onboard RGB (ESP-043) reflects the resulting state (armed blink → running).
- [ ] Product build (Touch) unaffected: `HAS_BUTTONS`-for-Mini does not pull LED pins or the
      two-button block into the Touch.
- [ ] A pure test for the short-vs-long gesture decision (mirror `test_status_rgb`) — the timing
      threshold is logic, not glue.

## Notes

- Pairs with **ESP-021** (enclosure + panel-mount button + TRS out) — this is the firmware half of
  the same "make the Mini a real box" story.
- Gesture grammar comes from the office-hours doc: press = play/stop, hold = realign. Keep it to
  one button; more controls belong in the app or a later "Performance" tier (arcade buttons).
- The quantized-press blink is load-bearing (a press does nothing for up to a bar; without the
  armed blink it reads as a dead button) — ESP-043 already provides it, which is why this ticket
  is input-only.

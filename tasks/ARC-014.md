## Problem

The two measurement glue files are near line-for-line twins:
`KitchenSync/main/link_measure_io.c:96-168` and `X32Link/link_measurement_io.cpp:45-120`.
Identical `run_action` 6-case dispatch, identical `run_all`, identical 4-step poll
ordering (incl. the `!active` early return, the peer scan, the per-pong `ended`
early-return), identical `now_us`. The **only** genuine per-target difference is ~4
socket primitives: open, send-to-ip:port, recv-one-nonblocking, flush (lwip vs
WiFiUDP; `htonl` vs `ip_from_be32`). The bug-prone ordering is duplicated and can
silently drift between boards.

## Solution (deepen)

`link_measure_pump(const LinkMeasureSocketOps* ops)` owns `run_action` + the 4-step
poll; each board supplies only a 4-function ops struct (`open/send/recv/flush`). Two
real adapters = a real seam; the policy exists once.

Deletion test: delete the pump and both boards lose the poll ordering — it
concentrates complexity, doesn't just move it.

## Notes

- The pump can be host-tested against a fake ops (in-memory socket) — the poll
  sequence becomes testable for the first time.
- No behaviour change intended; diff the two current polls first to confirm they're
  byte-equivalent before collapsing (they should be after this session's changes).

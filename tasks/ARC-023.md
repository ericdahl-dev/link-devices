## Problem

`/status` today reports **lifetime scalars**: `max_gap`, `max_work`, `overruns`, `bursts`,
`dropped`, and the per-stage worst case (`wbeats`/`wclock`/`wtport`). Those are exactly the
numbers that caught ESP-018, and they earned their keep.

But they answer *"has anything ever gone wrong?"* -- not *"what went wrong, and when?"*

- A max is **sticky**. One 40 ms stall during boot pins `max_gap` at 40000 forever, and every
  subsequent healthy hour is invisible behind it.
- A counter has **no time axis**. `overruns: 7` cannot distinguish seven faults spread over a
  week from seven in one bad second during the chorus.
- Nothing **correlates**. Was the stall during an OTA? A web save? A Link tempo change? A WiFi
  reconnect? The scalars cannot say, so every field report becomes a guessing game.

## Why now

**Dan's unit is about to be in the field**, driving a Korg ER-1 in someone else's room, on
someone else's network, with no analyzer on it and no serial console attached. The entire bench
methodology this project relies on -- *measure, don't guess* -- **does not travel with the
device.**

When Dan says "it stuttered once during the second song," the honest answer today is: we cannot
tell you anything. `overruns` will read some integer. That is the whole diagnostic surface.

Every real bug found this cycle was found by *instrumenting and then reading the numbers*. Ship a
device with no way to read the numbers and the method stops working exactly when it starts
mattering most.

## Solution

A small **ring buffer of the last N clock faults**, dumped on `/status`. Each entry records what
the scalars throw away:

- **when** it happened (uptime ms -- no RTC needed, and none should be added for this)
- **what kind**: gap (never scheduled) vs work (blocked inside the tick) -- the distinction that
  ESP-018 turned on, and the first question anyone will ask
- **how bad**: the microseconds
- **how many pulses were dropped**, if the realign tripped
- **the per-stage split** on a work fault (`wbeats`/`wclock`/`wtport`), since that is what
  identified the preempted call last time

N small -- 16 or 32. It is a diagnostic, not a log.

### Hard constraint: this must not become the bug it is looking for

The first version of the ESP-018 probe **logged on every overrun**. The log was a blocking UART
write, which delayed the next tick, which logged again: **3,945 phantom overruns in 40 seconds.**
It manufactured the fault it measured, and would have sent us chasing a scheduler ghost.

So, non-negotiable:

- The writer task **only stores into the ring**. Fixed-size, pre-allocated, no malloc, no
  formatting, no locks, no `ESP_LOGx`, no string work.
- All rendering happens on the **web/status side**, off the RT path.
- Writing an entry must cost a handful of stores. If it costs more than that, it is wrong.

The ring is a *record* of faults. It must never be a *source* of them.

## Scope

- [ ] Pure ring-buffer module in `X32Link/` -- the shared pure layer both other firmwares build
      from (P4 CMakes against it, Touch symlinks it), so all three devices inherit it by
      construction. Host-tested: push past capacity and confirm it wraps and keeps the *newest*
      N, that a fresh instance is empty (`clock_ticker.reset()` already taught this codebase that
      an un-zeroed field on a stack-allocated struct reads garbage), and that entries come back
      newest-first.
- [ ] Writer tasks push an entry on a gap/work overrun or a dropped-pulse realign. Stores only.
- [ ] `/status` renders the ring. Keep the existing scalars -- they are cheap and they are what
      you glance at. The ring is what you read when a glance says something is wrong.
- [ ] Wire on **all three** firmwares. X32Link has no tick probe at all today -- that is ARC-024,
      and it should land first or alongside, since there is nothing to feed the ring from there.
- [ ] Confirm on the bench that enabling the ring changes **nothing**: same `max_gap`, same
      `overruns`, same jitter on the analyzer. If the instrumentation moves the numbers, the
      instrumentation is the bug.

## Out of scope

- Persisting faults across reboot. NVS writes stall the cache and freeze both cores -- writing
  flash from a fault handler in a 1 ms RT loop would be a spectacular own-goal. RAM only.
- Remote/push telemetry. Nothing phones home. `/status` is a page the owner loads.
- An RTC or wall-clock timestamps. Uptime is sufficient and free.

## Further notes

Pairs with ARC-024 (X32Link has no probe to feed this from) and ESP-020 (the Touch cannot be

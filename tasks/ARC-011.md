## Problem

The gossiped Link timeline is a single last-writer-wins global `s_timeline`
(`link_protocol.c:38`, read raw by `link_proto_timeline` at `:163`). It fans out to
TWO independent consumers with no shared notion of "settled":

- the epoch detector `link_session_on_timeline` (`link_measurement_session.c:11`),
  now debounced for P4-028; and
- the beat/phase math — `beat_source_step` (`beat_source.c:35`) on the P4 and
  `tempo_source` (`X32Link/tempo_source.cpp:100`) on the S3.

The P4-028 debounce holds the last-good origin **trapped inside `LinkSession`**; the
beat consumer never sees it, so during the settle window it still computes phase from
a joining peer's junk `time_origin` / `mpb` / `beat_origin`. Two owners of "is the
timeline trustworthy now?", no shared truth. This is the split-brain gap where
P4-028's residual glitch lives.

Deletion test: delete `link_session_on_timeline` and the beat math is byte-for-byte
unchanged — the two consumers don't actually share epoch state, so it isn't one
module yet.

## Solution (deepen)

Promote the store into a pure `SessionTimeline` module that owns arbitration + the
settle / hold-last-good debounce (the P4-028 logic moves here from the session),
exposing one read: `session_timeline_settled(LinkTimeline* out)`. Both the epoch
detector and the beat math read *that*. The junk-window reaches neither consumer, on
both boards. This is the remaining half of **P4-028 (B)**, done once and shared.

- Move the debounce (settle window, candidate tracking) out of `LinkSession` into
  `SessionTimeline`; the session consumes the settled timeline like everyone else.
- Debounce on all three fields the beat math reads, not just `time_origin_us`.
- Host-test the settle/hold-last-good in isolation (the interface is the test
  surface); keep the existing epoch tests, retargeted at the new owner.

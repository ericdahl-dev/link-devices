## Problem (architecture review candidate B)

`P4Hub/main/p4hub_main.c` `clock_out_task` (the 1 ms `while(1)` loop) holds the
**beat-source policy** in untested glue:
- Choosing phase-locked `link_phase_beats_now` vs free-run `beat_clock_advance`
  (the `xform.valid` branch).
- The re-prime-on-switch: `if (locked != phase_locked)` resets the tick + click
  grids, and restarts `beat_clock` on drop to free-run.

The ingredients are all pure + tested; the *policy that composes them* is not.
A regression (invert the edge, drop the `beat_clock_reset`) is a phase glitch
visible only on hardware â€” exactly the class ADR-0003 exists to catch.

## Solution

A pure `beat_source.c`: `step({have_session, xform, timeline, now}, state) ->
{beats, basis, reprime}`. The task loop applies the result. The freeâ†”locked
transition becomes a table-testable state machine.

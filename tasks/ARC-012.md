## Problem

The P4 has the right shape: a single pure, host-tested `ks_config_set(key,value)`
(`KitchenSync/main/ks_config.c:58`) + `ks_form.c` that both the web and `/live`
handlers delegate to. The S3 has **no such owner** — field parse + range checks are
hand-rolled twice, in different shapes:

- web: `X32Link/web_config.cpp:340` (`handle_save`, imperative `toInt()` + inline
  bounds); and
- touch: `X32Link/touch_ui.c:60` (`ui_apply_settings_tap`, enum-driven, its own inline
  clamps — e.g. quantum `<16/>1` at `:75-78` vs `config_validate`'s 1-16 at
  `app_config.c:44`).

Same rules, two encodings → they drift. Only the model→slot clamp was shared
(`config_set_model`, LNK-032) — the seam is recognized but half-closed.

## Solution (deepen)

Mirror the P4: extract a pure `app_config_set(AppConfig*, field, value)` with per-field
ranges, host-tested like `ks_config`. Both `handle_save` and `ui_apply_settings_tap`
call it; `config_validate` stays the final gate. Per-field range logic stops living in
two copies.

## Notes

- Low risk: pure extraction of a pattern the P4 already proves; no glue/hardware
  change. Locality win — "what a valid quantum is" gets one home.
- Add `test/test_app_config_set.c` (interface is the test surface).
